The 10 Best Computer Monitors

The 10 Best Computer Monitors

▩ By: Mansour Oussama
▩ Jan 16 2015

Let's face it, most people who buy a PC don't necessarily give much thought to the monitor it comes with; or they stick with the display they already have. You know, when 1,024 by 786 was considered high-res. We're here to tell you that it's time to upgrade your view. After all, a large part of your day is spent staring at your screen. But what you should get depends on what you need. Are you on a tight budget? Do you need to see several windows open side by side on your screen? Planning on using the monitor for work and play? We at PCMag dissect the morass of monitors on the market today to give you the best ones you can get.

Regardless of the kind of monitor you are looking for, a few general factors come into play. First is price. Budget monitors (those less than $200 or 27-inch displays that retail for about $300) lack such features as USB ports and an adjustable stand, but performance is fine for most entertainment and business purposes. But if color and gray-scale accuracy are important to you, then you'll need to spend more. At the other end are your high-end models, which target graphics design and photography pros. At around $2,000 and up, these are usually large (30-inch) screens with In-Plane Switching (IPS) technology, and stands that can adjust height, tilt, and swivel. Gamers need more specialized monitors, definitely ones with great pixel response rate (think 2 milliseconds, gray-to-gray), while the average user will be fine with 5 milliseconds (gray-to-gray).

In terms of size, monitors run the gamut from 15 to 30 inches (measured diagonally). The rule of thumb is, the bigger the monitor, the more you pay. Typically, people look for monitors in the 22- to 24-inch range.

As of late, the kind of operating system you use has become a factor to consider. Those who purchase a system with Windows 8 will want to consider the new touch-screen monitors that have come to market. A touch-screen monitor will let you take advantage of Win8's new user. Arguably, you can always use keyboard shortcuts to navigate your way through Win 8, but a touch-screen display will make getting used to the new OS a bit easier. For more on what you should look for when purchasing a monitor, check out our buying guide. We even help you choose a monitor for graphic design and photo editing.

Whatever your need or budget, there is a computer monitor out there that's just the right fit for you. But we winnow down the selection with the best monitors on the market today. Check out our picks below.

HP DreamColor Z27x

The HP DreamColor Z27x professional-grade 27-inch monitor that delivers outstanding performance across the board, and is equipped with plenty of features, including built-in calibration software, and a six-port USB hub.

NEC MultiSync EA244UHD

The NEC MultiSync EA244UHD is a 24-inch 4K monitor that delivers outstanding color and gray-scale performance, crisp UHD imagery, and numerous connectivity options for its high price.

NEC MultiSync PA322UHD

The NEC MultiSync PA322UHD is a massive 32-inch, professional-grade Ultra-High-Definition (UHD) monitor that delivers excellent color, gray-scale, and 4K performance.

Acer B286HK

The Acer B286HK is a reasonably priced 28-inch UHD monitor that delivers a highly detailed image, good gray-scale reproduction, and solid gaming performance. It's loaded with features, too, including an ergonomic stand, a USB hub, and advanced color settings.

Acer H276HL

The Acer H276HL is an attractive 27-inch monitor that uses In-Plane Switching (IPS) technology to deliver accurate color and gray-scale performance. At just under $250 this is one of the best values around.

AOC E1759Fwu

The AOC E1759Fwu is a 17-inch, USB-powered portable monitor featuring a slim, easy-to-carry design, solid image quality, and bright, accurate colors.

AOC i2473PWM

The top-performing AOC i2473PWM is a stylish 24-inch monitor featuring a powerful speaker system. It offers accurate colors and good gray-scale performance.

Asus PB287Q

The Asus PB287Q is a reasonably priced 28-inch Ultra High-Definition monitor that delivers stunning 4K imagery and handles fast motion with aplomb.

Asus ROG Swift PG278Q

The Asus ROG Swift PG278Q is a 27-inch WQHD monitor designed for gamers. It uses Nvidia's G-Sync technology and a 144Hz refresh rate to deliver super-smooth motion performance, and it offers some neat gaming features.

Dell UltraSharp UZ2315H

The Dell UltraSharp UZ2315H is a well-appointed 23-inch monitor designed for video conferencing and multimedia use. Its overall performance and robust feature set put it at the top of its class.
